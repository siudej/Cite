Search features

There is a choice of ArXiv, MathSciNet and Zentralblatt for searching. The latter two can also use MRef to validate a reference. An all inclusive search is also possible.
  • Search supports au:, ti:, py:(yr:,dt:) so:(jo:) and any: modifiers
    (for author, title, publish year, journal and any fields). Other modifiers specific to ArXiv, MathSciNet or zbMATH should work also.
  • Publish year field supports ranges (ex. py:2001-2007) and before/after (ex. py:<2000)
  • Certain inputs at the beginning of a new line trigger autoguessing. E.g. a year or range will be interpreted as publication year, last, f* will be treated as author, book OR b, journal OR j, proceedings OR p OR a as type of publication
  • Words do not need to match in the order they are typed and as phrases. This is not the standard behavior of MathSciNet. The behavior is similar to most web search engines.
  • Logical operators and, or can be inserted between words or fields
  • New lines are automatically interpreted as new fields
  • Lines are connected by AND, unless a different connector is given at the end of the line

Search examples

  • isoperimetric inequalities Polya: search for all the words anywhere in the reference
  • isoperimetric inequalities au:Polya: now Polya must be an author
  • ti:isoperimetric inequalities any:Polya: Polya can appear anywhere, but the rest must be in the title
  • Polya py:1950-1960: publication year between 1950 and 1960, Polya anywhere. py: could be ommited if years in new line
  • Polya, G*: MathSciNet assumes this is author search
  • Polya, G* ti:isoperimetric: still author search, but with isoperimetric in title

Reference numbers

  • There is a slight chance that Zbl and MR numbers would not match the same reference, assuming MRef was used with zbMATH.
  • However, DOI always matches MR, if MR is available, even if not shown. Otherwise it matches Zbl number.
  • The following Latex commands can be used to generate hyperlinked numbers. 
     \newcommand{\mref}[1]{%
 \href{http://www.ams.org/mathscinet-getitem?mr=#1}{#1}}

 \newcommand{\arxiv}[1]{%
 \href{http://front.math.ucdavis.edu/#1}{ArXiv:#1}}

 \newcommand{\doi}[1]{%
 \href{http://dx.doi.org/#1}{doi:#1}}

 \newcommand{\zbl}[1]{%
 \href{http://zbmath.org/?q=an#1}{Zbl #1}}
  • For non-hyperlinked numbers use 
     \newcommand{\mref}{}
 \newcommand{\arxiv}[1]{ArXiv:#1}
 \newcommand{\doi}[1]{doi:#1}
 \newcommand{\zbl}[1]{Zbl #1}
